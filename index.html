How Solar Energy Works

Solar panels convert sunlight into electricity through photovoltaic cells. When sunlight hits these cells, it creates an electric field across layers of silicon, generating direct current (DC) electricity. An inverter then converts this DC electricity into alternating current (AC) that powers your home.

1. Sunlight Absorption

High-efficiency solar panels absorb photons from sunlight, even on cloudy days.

2. Energy Conversion

Photovoltaic cells convert sunlight into direct current (DC) electricity.

3. Power Conversion

An inverter converts DC electricity into usable AC electricity for your home.

4. Energy Distribution

Electricity powers your home, with excess energy stored in batteries or fed back to the grid.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do solar panels last?

Most solar panels come with a 25-year performance warranty and can continue producing electricity for 30-40 years. The efficiency gradually decreases by about 0.5% per year.

What maintenance do solar panels require?

Solar systems require minimal maintenance. We recommend occasional cleaning (especially in dusty areas) and annual inspections to ensure optimal performance.

Do solar panels work during power outages?

Standard grid-tied systems shut down during outages for safety. However, with battery backup systems, you can maintain power during outages.

How much roof space do I need?

A 5kW system typically requires about 25-35 square meters, while a 10kW system needs 50-70 square meters, depending on panel efficiency.

What financing options are available?

We offer various options including cash purchases, solar loans with low interest rates, and lease/PPA agreements with no upfront costs.
